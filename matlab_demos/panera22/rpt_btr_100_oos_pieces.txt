The results described here correspond to a small change in how I calculate initial predictions.

When I calculate an initial prediction I can choose to keep the space
between is-data and oos-data small.

A smaller space should give me better results.

The price I pay is that software takes much longer to run.

The way I make the space smaller is to divide the oos-data into more pieces.

Initially I divided the oos-data into 10 pieces.

The result of that decision is described here:
/a/ks/b/matlab/panera22/rpt_btr_10_oos_pieces.txt


This file,
/a/ks/b/matlab/panera22/rpt_btr_100_oos_pieces.txt
describes the results connected to my decision to divide the oos-data into 100 pieces.

When I compare the 10-pieces-decision to the 100-pieces-decision,
I see the 10-pieces-decision give better results.

This good news.

Predictions derived from 10-oos-data-pieces require much less time to calculate.
